There are many different kinds of water ecosystems:
Oceans, Rivers, Lakes, Ponds.
Each one has unique fish, insects, wildlife, and plants that depend on each other in different ways.

Click On An Ecosystem Below To Start Exploring!

Oceans are vast bodies of saltwater that cover most of the Earth's surface. They are home to a wide variety of plants and animals, including whales, sharks, and coral reefs.

Rivers are bodies of freshwater that flow from high ground to low ground. They are important because they provide water for plants, animals, and people. Rivers are home to many different types of fish, such as salmon and trout.

Lakes are bodies of freshwater that are surrounded by land. They can be big or small, and they are important habitats for many different types of plants and animals. Some common animals found in lakes include fish, turtles, and ducks.

Ponds are smaller bodies of freshwater, smaller than lakes, that are also surrounded by land. They are usually shallow and are home to many different types of plants and animals, such as frogs and dragonflies.
