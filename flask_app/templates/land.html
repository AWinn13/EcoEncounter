There are many different kinds of land ecosystems:
Forests, Grassland, Desert. Tundra, and Wetland.
Each one has its own unique plants and animals that depend on each other in different ways.

Click On An Ecosystem Below To Start Exploring!

Forests are areas with lots of trees and plants. There are many different types of forests all around the world, but some of the most common include tropical rainforests, temperate forests, and boreal forests. Forests provide homes for many different types of animals, and they also help to clean the air we breathe.

Grasslands are areas where there is lots of grass, but not many trees. There are two main types of grasslands: savannas and prairies. Savannas have tall grass and a few scattered trees, while prairies have shorter grass and no trees at all. Grasslands are home to many different types of animals, including large grazing animals like bison and zebras.

Deserts are areas where there is very little rainfall and very few plants. There are many different types of deserts all around the world, but they all have one thing in common: they are very dry! Because there is so little water in the desert, it can be a tough place for animals to survive. However, some animals, like camels and desert tortoises, have adapted to life in the desert and are able to thrive there.

Tundra is a type of ecosystem that is found in very cold places, like the Arctic. In the tundra, the ground is frozen most of the year, and there are very few trees or plants. Despite the harsh conditions, many animals are able to live in the tundra, including polar bears, caribou, and arctic foxes.

Wetlands are areas that are covered in water for part of the year. They can be found near rivers, lakes, and oceans. Wetlands are important because they help to filter water and prevent flooding. They are also home to many different types of animals, including ducks, frogs, and alligators.
